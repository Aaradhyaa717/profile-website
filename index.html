Research

Honors Thesis

Sep 2025 – Apr 2026

Advisor: Prof. Claire Wardle

“Swipe, Stress, Stuck: Exploring People's Attitudes About Online Dating as Observed in Reddit and Survey Responses”

I spent a year trying to answer a simple question: what keeps people in dating apps? I ran a 175-person survey of Cornell students and scraped 5,833 posts across 10 dating-related subreddits, then layered metaphor coding, VADER, and GoEmotions on top of both datasets to triangulate how people actually talk about the experience versus how they say they feel about it.

The pattern held across both datasets: people experience harm, rationalize it, convince themselves they have more control over the algorithm than they do, and keep using the app. I'm calling it the Circle of Harm Tolerance. Some of the numbers behind it: 60% of survey respondents had experienced at least one form of harm on a dating app, but 71% of them never reported it. And when people described what online dating felt like, 23.88% reached for labor metaphors — not just the market and game comparisons the existing literature mostly talks about.

The thesis is a first step toward understanding how people rationalize harm in online spaces, and how that rationalization can be disrupted. It also points to the need for more research on the role of labor metaphors in people's experiences of online dating.
